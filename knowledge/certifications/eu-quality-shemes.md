# EU quality schemes

The European Union maintains three primary quality schemes for agricultural
products and foodstuffs. All are administered through the eAmbrosia register
(`ec.europa.eu/agriculture/eambrosia/`).

## PDO — Protected Designation of Origin

- Italian: **DOP** (Denominazione di Origine Protetta)
- Spanish: **DOP** (Denominación de Origen Protegida)
- French: **AOP** (Appellation d'Origine Protégée)
- Portuguese: **DOP** (Denominação de Origem Protegida)

**Strictest tier.** Product must be:
1. **Produced** in a defined geographical area
2. **Processed** in that same area
3. **Prepared** in that same area

All three steps must occur in the named region using recognized know-how.

**Examples:**
- Parmigiano Reggiano (Emilia-Romagna, Italy)
- Roquefort (France)
- Queso Manchego (Castile-La Mancha, Spain)
- Azeite de Moura (Portugal)

## PGI — Protected Geographical Indication

- Italian: **IGP** (Indicazione Geografica Protetta)
- Spanish: **IGP** (Indicación Geográfica Protegida)
- French: **IGP** (Indication Géographique Protégée)
- Portuguese: **IGP** (Indicação Geográfica Protegida)

**Less strict than PDO.** Product must be linked to the geographical area in
at least **one** of: production, processing, or preparation. Other stages may
occur elsewhere.

**Examples:**
- Bresaola della Valtellina (Lombardy, Italy)
- Jamón de Trevélez (Andalusia, Spain)
- Pollo de Bresse (France) — also AOC for some labels
- Borrego do Baixo Alentejo (Portugal)

## TSG — Traditional Specialities Guaranteed

- Italian: **STG** (Specialità Tradizionale Garantita)

**Highlights traditional character.** Does NOT bind product to geographical
origin — instead, certifies that recipe and method follow tradition.

**Examples:**
- Mozzarella (TSG, distinct from Mozzarella di Bufala Campana DOP)
- Jamón Serrano (Spain)
- Pizza Napoletana (TSG)

## National quality schemes (additional layer)

Beyond EU schemes, several countries operate national certifications that
Iriska also recognizes:

- **Suisse Garantie** (Switzerland) — origin and quality assurance
- **Label Rouge** (France) — superior quality (not always tied to origin)
- **AOC** (France, pre-EU framework) — historical designation, often
  upgraded to AOP
- **DOC/DOCG** (Italy, wines) — Denominazione di Origine Controllata e
  Garantita; Iriska does not actively trade wine in Y1 but catalog
  references DOC/DOCG entries

## Verification on Iriska

When a supplier claims PDO/PGI/TSG status for a product, Iriska:
1. Looks up the GI name in eAmbrosia register
2. Confirms the supplier's legal entity is among the registered producers OR
   is an authorized distributor (verified via supplier-uploaded contract)
3. Checks expiration / suspension status
4. Records verification result in `events_log` with full audit trail
