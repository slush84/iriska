# General FAQ

> **Status:** Skeleton. Populate as common questions emerge from suppliers and buyers.

## What is Iriska?

Iriska is a B2B procurement platform connecting European producers of protected
and heritage products with HoReCa buyers (hotels, restaurants, catering),
specialty retail, and online retail across Spain, Italy, France, Portugal,
Netherlands, Belgium, Germany, and growing.

## Who are buyers on Iriska?

Restaurants, hotels, catering companies, specialty food retailers, and online
retailers sourcing premium Mediterranean products: PDO/PGI cheeses, Iberian
charcuterie, single-estate olive oils, conservas, heritage rices and grains,
saffron and herbs, honeys, and other quality-certified categories.

## Who are suppliers on Iriska?

European producers and authorized distributors of protected designation
products. Iriska does not list industrial or commodity goods — listing requires
PDO, PGI, TSG, AOP, DOP, AOC, Suisse Garantie, or comparable quality-certified
status.

## How does Iriska make money?

Commission deducted from supplier price (Wildberries-style architecture):
8% on B2B orders, 15% on B2C orders. No listing fees, no platform subscription,
no exclusivity required. Commission charged only on accepted, paid orders.

## Where is Iriska based?

Iriska B.V. is registered in the Netherlands. Operational team is distributed
across Amsterdam (NL) and Barcelona (ES). Customer-facing language is initially
English, with local-language rollouts in Phase 5.

## What products does Iriska currently list?

The catalog covers 11 categories: Charcuterie, Cheese & Dairy, Olive Oil, Olives,
Seafood Conservas, Spices Salt & Herbs, Rice Pasta & Grains, Honey, Sweets &
Pastry, Vinegars & Condiments, Preserves. Wine is on the catalog as reference
but not actively traded in Y1 (operational complexity around licensing).
